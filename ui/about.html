Securing Africa’s Mobile Money Future with AI

Mobile Money is the primary financial system for hundreds of millions of people across Africa. From daily payments to salaries and remittances, it powers economic activity across the continent. However, this success has come with a growing cost.

Recent studies estimate that Africa loses over $1.5 billion annually to mobile money fraud, largely driven by social engineering scams, phishing links, vishing calls, SIM-swap attacks, and agent-assisted fraud. Traditional transaction-only fraud systems detect threats too late — often after funds are already gone.

Why MAIFDS Was Built

MAIFDS (MoMo AI Fraud Defense System) was designed to address how fraud actually happens in African mobile money ecosystems. Instead of analyzing transactions alone, MAIFDS correlates calls, clicks, messages, and transaction behavior to detect fraud intent early — before financial loss occurs.

Powered by Huawei MindSpore

MAIFDS leverages Huawei MindSpore to train and deploy real-time AI models that operate at telecom scale. These models work alongside deterministic rule engines to ensure high accuracy, explainability, and low false positives — critical requirements for financial and telecom environments.
